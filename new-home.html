New Home

Study project solarpunk Action RPG game

New Home is a solarpunk Action RPG with a focus on resource management where Earth has been blown up by invading forces and you have to make one of the last human colonies thrive. The story would follow a settlement formed by humans as they explore a new planet to survive and, eventually, defend themselves from the same aliens that blew up Earth.

The players would have to go into expeditions that would start at the beginning of non-linear, circular levels to gather resources, which would be used for upgrading the main character and unlock shortcuts to get iteratively further. The settlement would also be upgradable, unlocking new features and dialogues with the refugees and changing the appearance of the settlement to go from cold and utilitarian houses to a solarpunk village.

Working on New Home

New Home was the final project I made during my studies in Level Up. We were tasked to create a GDD as well as other game design documents relevant to the project. At that point, I had never seen a complete GDD before so there was a lot of trying and failing. We also had to do a pitch of our projects to the rest of the class and some members of the academy, which can be seen here.

The project was a success, I learnt a lot from it and it was a nice first experience. But it wasn't finished. Some months later, I did a post-mortem of New Home to analyze the development process, note additional work needed to be done to create a vertical slice and the lessons learnt. For now, the development of this project has been terminated with no plans of continuing. But who knows, maybe down the future, when I'm ready, I'll take off the dust of the GDD and continue it.

Level Up

Level Up is a game dev hub that offers learning programs for game development as well as an incubator for new-founded teams. After my Computer Science studies, Level Up was my next stop after I met them at Indie Dev Day 2023. 8 months later, I finished my final project and made the final presentation to graduate from the Conceptualization and Documentation of Video Games' bootcamp, ready to start my game dev career.
