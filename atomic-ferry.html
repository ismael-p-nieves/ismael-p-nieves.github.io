Atomic Ferry

Indie dev team

Atomic Ferry is an indie dev team composed of some of my friends and me with the objective of doing small projects and participate in Game Jams to gain experience, create portfolio and have a good time together. We are currently working with Godot, but we intend to expand our knowledge as future projects require.

During my team at college, I met wonderful and passionate people that nowadays I have the honor to call friends. When thinking about doing games on my own, it just felt right to ask them to join me. After spending some time learning about Godot, organizing small events to showcase our work and defining a structure for the team, we are now ready to start our own journey as game devs.

Godot

We are currently focused on using Godot as our starting language. Every member of Atomic Ferry has a background in Computer Science so we could have adapted to any language, and even some of us had already worked with Unity, but we decided to settle on Godot for its simplicity; plus, we already had access to resources for learning Godot. In the future, we will be checking out other engines like Unreal, but for now Godot will be our way to go.

All of us have individually taken the GameDev.tv courses for Godot over the course of a month. The courses consisted of multiple small projects, and to incite creativity and discovery, we all had to add a bit more to those projects and then share it with the rest of the team. Some designed boss fights, other multiplayer modes, or even changing the game completely. It was a fun and interesting experience that made us learn and bond as a team.

So what now?

Now that we have learnt Godot, we are ready to embark on a new project. We will be working on a small game, and over the course of multiple months, we will be learning how to think as a team, organize as a team and work as a team. In my case, I will be taking the role of Game and Level Designer, as well as the Product Manager and 2D artist. So stay tuned to see what's coming!
